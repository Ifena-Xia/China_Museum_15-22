# China Museum Investment Analysis (2016–2022)

**Live site:** https://ifena-xia.github.io/China_Museum_15-22/

An empirical study of museum investment patterns across China's Eastern, Central, and Western regions from 2016 to 2022. Rather than using the conventional per capita metric, this project introduces subsidy and expenditure per square meter of museum floor space as a more precise measure of funding intensity — one that controls for the large disparities in museum size and population density across regions.

## Methods

Correlation analysis (Pearson and Spearman's Rank), log-transformed provincial heatmaps, regional trend visualization. Interactive dashboard built with Python (Plotly Dash), hosted on PythonAnywhere.

## Data

- Statistical Yearbook of Cultural Relics of China (SYCRC)
- Statistical reports published by the Ministry of Culture and Tourism (MCT), 2016–2022

## Key finding

Despite intuitive expectations, GDP per capita shows no strong or statistically significant correlation with museum subsidy or expenditure per square meter. Wealthier provinces tend to build larger museums rather than invest more intensively per unit of space, suggesting that government subsidy distribution is partially decoupled from provincial economic development.
